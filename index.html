My wife and I have a (pipe)dream of putting an addition onto our home. The project started out as converting an existing screen porch into a four-season sunroom. The idea has now grown into adding an extra garage stall or two and a new master suite to the back of the house. Ignoring the matter of architectural feasability, all this talk got me thinking. How much is an extra bedroom, bathroomm, ~500 sq ft. and 2 garage stalls worth? How much value would a project of this add to our home. Would the added value offset the costs of construction.

This is the idea that this project was born from. My goal was to build a data scraper to gather real estate listings from the past year for the surrounding neighborhoods. I built the datascraper in R and collected the data from theMLSonline.com on March 9, 2015. There are some limitations to the data set. The website only posts the list price for each property, as opposed to the sales price.

There are better sites out there for this kind of data, but the barrier is higher. I have been working on a webscraper in Python to gather data from the city of Minneapolis' property details database. The data from this website is much richer, and will eventually lead to much more interesting results. As of right now I have yet to work the kinks out of this system, and so I am left with my preliminary data set from theMLSonline. What follows is an exploratory analysis of this data.

Which model is better? Its hard to say at this point as theory does not really point me in one specific direction. A case could be made for the loglistprice model because it is somewhat natural to talk about housing prices in terms of percentages. But one could easily make the argument that dollar increase are also natural. At this point, there is not much else I can say on the matter until I dig deeper into the existing data. I am currently building a web-scraping app that will gather data off of Minneapolis' property details website to analyze and hopefully come up with some deeper insights. In subsequent posts I will include other regressors and see if I can't get a good predictive model for how much value increase an addition would provide.
